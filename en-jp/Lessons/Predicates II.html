Predicates Part II

On part 1, we've taken a look at the characteristics of these predicatables:
  • Verb
  • Adjective

But we are still left with how to make these as predicate:

  • Noun Attributive
  • Adjective Attributive

Basically, it's just like in English.

  1. It (is a dog).
  2. It (is quiet).

Noun Attribute

  • 犬「いぬ」dog
  • 猫「ねこ」cat
  • ネズミmouse
  • 学生「がくせい」student
  • 医者「いしゃ」doctor
  • 歯医者「はいしゃ」dentist

    To make a noun describe the subject as a predicate we attribute it to ある.
    And to attach an atttibute to ある, use the particle で.
  • 医者である
    He is a doctor.
    • More commonly, instead or using である, です is used
  • 学生です
    I am a student
    • More casually, だ is used.
  • 犬だ
    It's a dog.

Adjective Attribute

Same as with nouns.
  • 好き「すき」Pleasant / Favourable (usually translated as like)
  • 有名「ゆうめい」Famous
  • 静「しずか」Quiet
  • 便利「べんり」Convenient
  • 大切「たいせつ」Important / Valued / Dear
  • 上手「じょうず」Skilful / Good at
  • 下手「へた」Unskilful / Bad at
  • 必要「ひつよう」Necessary
  • 素敵「すてき」Amazing
  • まじめSerious

  • 必要である
    It is important. (necessary)
  • 好きです
    It is pleasant. (I like it)
    It is my favourite.
  • 素敵だ
    It's amazing.

What is the difference between the adjectives in the last lesson and the adjective in this one?

In the last lesson, everything is conjugable. Nouns and this group of adjectives are not. That's why we have to attach the to the verb ある so that they can be conjugated.

If you have noticed, these adjectives do not end in い. It is the い in that adjective group that is the conjugable part.
