Predicates Part II

On part 1, we've taken a look at the characteristics of these predicatables:
  • Verb
  • Adjective

But we are still left withon how to make as predicate:

  • Adjective Attributive
  • Nominal Attributive

Basically, it's just like in English.

  1. The dog (is cute).
  2. Tanaka-san (is a student).

Verb

A verb always ends in う段「だん」(kana within the う row)
  • 好「すき」Like
  • 有名「ゆうめい」Famous
  • 静「しずか」Quiet
  • 便利「べんり」Convenient
  • 大切「たいせつ」Important, precious
  • 上手「じょうず」Skilful, good at
  • 下手「へた」Unskilful, not good at
  • 必要「ひつよう」Necessary
  • 立派「りっぱ」Splendid
  • まじめSerious
No verb ends in ず,づ,ふ,ぷ,ゆ

Adjectives

In one group of adjectives, all end in い (the kana い - not い段)
  • 楽しい「たのしい」fun
  • 嬉しい「うれしい」happy
  • 悲しい「かなしい」sad
  • 美味しい「おいしい」delicious
  • 可愛い「かわいい」cute
  • 暑い「あつい」hot (weather)
  • 熱い「あつい」hot (object)
  • 寒い「さむい」cold (weather)
  • 冷たい「つめたい」cold (object)
  • 良い「いい」good
