Course Overview

Predicates

Last lesson, we've learnt that a predicate in Japanese can be founded on
  • Verb
  • Adjective
And a complete sentence can be formed with just one word with these.

In this lesson, we will learn the characteristics of these predicatables.

Verb

A verb always ends in う段「だん」(kana within the う row)
  • 使う「つかう」use
  • 行く「いく」go
  • 泳ぐ「およぐ」swim
  • 出す「dasu」take out
  • 待つ「まつ」wait
  • 死ぬ「しぬ」die
  • 学ぶ「まなぶ」study
  • 読む「よむ」read
  • あるexist/there is
No verb ends in ず,づ,ふ,ぷ,ゆ

Adjectives

In one group of adjectives, all end in い (the kana い - not い段)
  • 楽しい「たのしい」fun
  • 嬉しい「うれしい」happy
  • 悲しい「かなしい」sad
  • 美味しい「おいしい」delicious
  • 可愛い「かわいい」cute
  • 暑い「あつい」hot (weather)
  • 熱い「あつい」hot (object)
  • 寒い「さむい」cold (weather)
  • 冷たい「つめたい」cold (object)
  • 良い「いい」good
