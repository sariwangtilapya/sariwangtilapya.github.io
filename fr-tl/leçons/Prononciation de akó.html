Pronunciation of akó

Akó has interesting pronunciation particularity, that its « a » is not pronounced if preceded by a word that ends in a vowel.

No, not that. Quite a lot of words do that actually and is then replaced with an apostrophe when writing. Most of the time people even drop the apostrophe.

But what interesting property it has is that after dropping its « a », it lengthens the vowel that the preceding word ends on.

But this doesn't apply only when the preceding word is "hindí¡"

  • Pūpuntá¯ 'ko
    I will go.
  • Hindí¡ akó pūpuntá
    I won't go.
  • Hindí¡ na¯ 'kó pūpuntá
    I won't go anymore
