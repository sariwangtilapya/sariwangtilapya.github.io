Epithet

An epithet is a direct description.

What is the difference between an epithet and a predicate

A predicate describes exclusively the subject and completes a sentence. While an epithet, can describe any unit or group in the sentence but does not complete the thought.

Take for example.

  • Masaráp ang mansanas. Apples are delicious.
    • vs.
  • Masaráp na mansanas Delicious apples

In the example, masaráp delicious described the subject but the epithet never completed the sentence.

The Epithet Connector (EpiCon)

In Tagalog, the described and the description is always linked with the EpiCon

NA

By default, the form is « na » but is subject these changes:

  • If the word before the epithet connector is a vowel, it becomes «ng» affixed to the end of it.
    • Puláng lobo red balloon
  • If the word before the epithet connector ends with «n», it is replaced with «ng».
    • balónng malalim deep well

Epithets usually can be placed either before or after but tend to place after.
But there are cases in which the epithet's placement is mandatory.

Epithets that MUST place before

  1. Number
    isáng oras one hour
  2. As an Auxiliary Predicate Unit when there is an 2P group* [pronouns and SPAM]
    Magalíng din siyáng coach He is a great coach as well.
Notice here that the EpiCon attached itself after the 2P group.

Epithets, Predicates, aren't they just adjectives? Why complicate it?

Actually, adjectives are just the words specialised for describing things.
The epithet and predicate classification designates the description scope.

Also, it's not just adjectives that can be descriptions.
A noun, a verb, a phrase, even a full sentence can be.

  • Haring Sejong King Sejong
  • boksingerong senador senator who is a boxer
  • tumatakbóng kabayo running horse
  • kabayong tumatakbo sa race track horse (that is) running on the race track
  • lalaking malakí ang ilóng man whose nose is big
