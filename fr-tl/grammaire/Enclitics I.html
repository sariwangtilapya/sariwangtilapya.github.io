na and pa

Expresses having passed the satisfaction of expectation or not.

Na and pa are opposites.

Usual translations for na: already, now

Usual translations for pa: still, more, even

Exploring expectations

Exploring expectation with the verb kain eat

  • What causes one to start eating?
    • being hungry
    • habit of eating at a specific time
  • What causes on to stop eating?
    • Being full
    • having something else to do like maybe work

If we employ «na», it means that expectations are met.

For example:

  • We are out with a friend and we start to get hungry, we ask our friend that we eat now. And besides, it's almost 9:00pm. The expectation of eating has now been satisfied and we invite them:
    • Kain na tayo Let's eat (already/now)
  • It's lunchtime at work. Today's free lunch turned out to be not filling enough. Besides, there's 20 minutes left before work resumes. The expectation of eating has not yet been satisfied and we invite them:
    • Kain pa tayo Let's eat (still/more)
