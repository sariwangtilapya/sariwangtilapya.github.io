Definiteness

  1. General
    2. When substantive is the general group

    Cakes are sweet. (cakes in general)
    Matamís ang cake.

  2. Definite
    3. When substantive is specific

    The cake is sweet. (the specific cake)
    Matamís yung cake.

    For EE nouns, the (+) version is used. The (-) version is only used for general things not specific things.

  3. Partitive / Definite-Partitive
    4. Part of a specific substantive

    I took (a slice of the) [some] cake. (a non-specific part of a specific cake)
    Kumuha ako nung cake.

  4. Indefinite
    5. A non-specific substative

    I bought a cake. (any one of all the cakes that exist.)
    Bumilí ako ng cake.

    For EE nouns, the (+) version is always used when talking about specific things.
