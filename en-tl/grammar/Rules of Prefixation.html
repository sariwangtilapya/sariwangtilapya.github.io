Prefixation

A prefix is an affix to attach before the word it modifies.

Common prefixes are

  • ma
    turns nouns into adjectives
  • ka
    ensemble
  • mag
    used for conjugations
  • pag
    turns verbs into nouns (supine)

ma gandá ka laró mag hilamos pag -alís¹
beauty play wash (the face) leave
beautiful playmate to wash (the face) departure

¹When the word to attach to a prefix that ends in a consonant starts with a vowel, a hypen shall be used to separate them.

Malleable « ng »

There are prefixes ending in « ng ».
Like:

  1. pang
    intended use for

The ending « ng » is malleable and changes form depending on the first letter of the word to prefix to.
If the first letter is included in:

  • [d, l, s, t]; ng → n
  • [b, p]; ng → m
pan lalaki pam babae pang- alís pang kuskos
men women going out scrub
for men for women for going out for scrubbing

Simple

until it's not.

Coalescing « ng »

The are words whose first letter interacts with the prefix ending « ng » in a peculiar way.

ng + b = m bilí mamilí go shopping
p pitás mamitás pick fruit from a plant
[vowel] ng isdá¡ mangisdá¡ to fish

[sh]² shokot scare panyokot for scaring others (gay slang)

²sh ← [sh, ch, sìy, sì, sy]
