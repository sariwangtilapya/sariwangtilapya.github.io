Hydration

A hydric word grows another head.
Hydration is one very important step in many conjugations and some declensions.
Remember to drink water.

Here's how to hydrate:

    • gawá¡
    • plakdá¡
    • iyák
    • hila
  1. Take the first syllable
    • ga
    • plak
    • i
    • hi
  2. Take its first consonant phoneme*
    • g
    • p
    • ø
    • h
  3. Take its vowel**
    • a
    • a
    • i
    • i
  4. Place 2 and 3 before the base
    • pupuntá
    • paplakdá¡
    • iiyák
    • hihila
  5. If the syllable next to the repeat syllable is short, lengthen the repeat syllable; otherwise, do nothing.
    • pūpuntá
    • pāplakdá¡
    • īiyák
    • hihila

*Consonant phoneme is one consonant sound unit. Sometimes they are written with more than one character.

  • ch sound can be spelt like these→[ch, tş, ty, tì, tìy]
  • sh→[sh, sy, sì, sìy]
  • j→[dy, dì, dìy]

**«i» could be a part of a consonant blend. This is «i» is not the syllable's vowel

    takbó → tātakbó
    balík → bābalík
    alís → āalís
    aral → mag-aaral
    challenge → ichachallenge
    prank → nampaprank
    blangko → nabablangko
    piano → nagpapiano
    dyaryo → dyadyaryuhin
