Hydration

From "Hydra", the mythological snake which can grow multiple heads. A hydric word grows another head.

Hydration is a very important step in many conjugations, declensions and other word modifications.
Remember to drink water.

Basically, hydration is a prefix whose form depends on the word it modifies.

Also a very important point about hydration, is that it always takes priority before any prefix. If a word must be hydrated, it must be hydrated first before adding another prefix.

There are three types of hydration:

  1. Normal
  2. Short
  3. Long

  1. Normal Hydration

    2. Here's how to hydrate:

    1. Take the first syllable
    2. Take its first consonant phoneme¹
    3. Take its vowel²
    4. If the first syllable of the base, lengthen the hydra's head.
    5. Prefix the hydra"s head

    ¹Tagalog used to be an all-consonant language. Consonants with some marks to modify its vowel or to remove it. The empty consonant is considered a consonant in many languages, this includes Tagalog.
    ²Semi-vowels [ì, y, ìy, ù, w, ùw] like in "piano", are not considered vowels. All syllables are in Tagalog only have one single vowel
    ³Consonant phoneme is one consonant sound unit. Sometimes they are written with more than one character.

    • ch sound can be spelt like these→[ch, tş, ty, tì, tìy]
    • sh→[sh, sy, sì, sìy]
    • j→[dy, dì, dìy]

  2. Short Hydration

    3. This passes through the same steps as normal hydration except that the length of the hydra's head is always short.
