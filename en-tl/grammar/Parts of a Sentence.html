Parts of a Sentence

A sentence requires two parts.

*With the exception of Impersonal Sentences and Snap Expressions. Explained below.
  1. Subject
    2. Subject is the entity being talked about in the sentence.
    A subject can take either form:
    1. Noun
    2. Pronoun
  2. Predicate
    3. The predicate is the main description of the subject in a sentence.
    It could take either of three functions:
    1. Verbal
      2. Tumakbò akò. I ran.
    2. Adjectival
      3. Maliít ang langgam. Ants are small.
    3. Nominal
      4. Prutas ang kamatis. Tomatoes are fruit.

There are two parts to a Predicate

The main predicate unit and its complements.

There are three types of MPU complements

They are:
  1. Objects
    2. I sent them an invitation via email.
    • an invitation - patient (undergoes the action)
    • them - beneficient (recieves the action)
    • via email - instrument (through which the action is realised)
  2. Auxiliary Predicate Unit
    3. An APU can be
    1. Adverb - if MPU is a verb
      2. Mabilis tumakbo itong kotseng ito. This car runs fast
    2. Adjective - if MPU is a noun
      3. Malaking isda ang tuna. Tuna is a big fish
    3. Auxiliaries and other verbs
      4. Kayang gawín ni Junjun 'yan. Junjun can do that.
      There can be more than one APU's
      Gustó ko siyáng makitang maglarô ng basketball I'd like to see him play basketball.
  3. Negative
    4. Hindi siyá marunong lumangóy. He can not swim.

Impersonal Sentences

These are sentences that don't take a subject because it's just something that happens. Usually just natural phenomena such as:

  • Umúulan. It's raining.
  • Lumílindol. There's an earthquake.
  • Bumábahà. It's flooding

Snap Expressions

These are one-unit expressions that can

  • imply urgency
    • Takbo! Run!
    • Tulong! Help!
    • Sunog! (There is) Fire!
  • call someone's attention
    • Uy! Hey!
    • Kuya! (Excuse me,) Sir! [calling the attention of a male stranger] [lit. Brother!]
  • etc.
