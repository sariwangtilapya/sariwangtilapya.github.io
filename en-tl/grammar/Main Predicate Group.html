Main Predicate Group

The Main Predicate Group is the main idea in the predicate
It can consist of:

  1. Left Predicate Unit
  2. Connected Predicate Group
    1. Connected Perceptive Verb
    2. Connected Verb

The Left Predicate Unit can be:

  1. Noun
    2. Muhammad Ali is a boxer.
    Boksingero si Muhammad Ali.
  2. Adjective
    3. Bopis is delicious.
    Masaráp ang bopis.
  3. Verb
    4. Grandpa jogs.
    Nagjojogging si lolo.

The Left Predicate Unit can be connective

  1. with a connected verb
    2. Peter can (is capable of) carry a sack of rice. (50kg)
    Kaya ni Peter magbuhát ng isáng sakong bigás.
    Sam heard Kate swear
    Narinig ni Sam magmurá si Kate.
  2. with a connected perceptive verb connected with another verb
    3. Mum wants to watch dad play basketball
    Gustó ni mama panoorin si papa maglaró¡ ng basketball

As you can see in the examples, the agent came before the connected verb. That is because Connected Predicate Units no longer belong in the Left Side but the Right.

Auxiliary verbs as they are called in English e.g. [want, can, must, needs] are Connective Left Exclusive Predicate Units (CLEPU) as they are exclusive to the left side and are connective.

Left Predicate Units can be qualified but qualifiers can not push them to the Right Side:

The elephant is a big animal.
Malakíng hayop ang elepante
Poodle is more intelligent
Mas matalino ang poodle.
Danny checks everyday for the plants to bear fruit.
Araw-araw chinecheck ni Danny na mamunga yung mgá halaman.
Efren Reyes is so good at playing billiards
Sobrang galíng ni Efren Bata¡ magbilyár.
He is too fond of smelling Marie fart.
Sobra nìyang hilig amuyíng umutót si Marie.

"Mas" and the word it modifies are inseparable and should be considered a single unit the Second Position Group can't come right after "mas"

Sobra and the word it modifies can either be separate or inseparable. It is more formal to separate them.
