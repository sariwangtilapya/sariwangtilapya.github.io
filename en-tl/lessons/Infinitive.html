Infinitive Form

There are three verb groups according to its conjugation.

  • mag verbs
  • um verbs
  • defective verbs

mag verbs' infinitive is formed by prefixing "mag" to the verb.

  • magtampó sulk

*if the verb starts with a vowel separate the »g« and the vowel with a hyphen » - «

  • mag-aral study

um verbs' infinitive is formed by infixing "um" after the first consonant of the verb.

  • kumantá sing

*if the verb starts with a vowel infix it after the non-existent first consonant –Filipino used to be an all consonant language– basically prefix it.

  • umiyák cry

**»ng« is a single letter in Filipino. Do not separate them

  • ngumitî smile

***for vowel blends such as sh, sy, ch, ts, dy –considerably a consonal sound unit by themselves place it after the consonant blend.

  • chumamba achieve something through chance

****for »r« or »l« blends more standard is to place after the first consonant. Some people infix after the »r« or »l«.

  • pumlakdâ suddenly fall flat face down

Defective verbs lack certain conjugations

There are two type of defective verbs

  1. ma verbs
  2. mang verbs

They both belong to the subjunctive/potential mood conjugations

They often express involition or non-control on either perspective of the doer or the undergoer. Like verbs such as

  • matulog ma- sleep
  • mang-away start a fight with
  • matisod trip/stumble
  • mangntisod or mantisod cause someone to trip or stumble
  • matuto learn/acquire knowledge
