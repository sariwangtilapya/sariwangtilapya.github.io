Attribute

An attribute is a direct description.

What is the difference between an attributes and a predicate

A predicate describes exclusively the subject and completes a sentence. While an attribute, can describe any unit or group in the sentence but does not complete the thought.

Take for example.

  • Masaráp ang mansanas. Apples are delicious.

    • vs.

  • Masaráp na mansanas Delicious apples

In the example, masaráp delicious described the subject but the attribute never completed the sentence.

The attribute connector

In Tagalog, the described and the description is always linked with the attribute connector

NA

By default, the form is » na « but is subject these changes:

  • If the word before the attribute connector is a vowel, it becomes «ng» affixed to the end of it.
    • Puláng lobo red balloon
  • If the word before the attribute connector ends with «n», it is replaced be «ng».
    • balónng malalim deep well

Attributes usually can be placed either before or after but tend to place after.

But there are cases in which attributes placement is mandatory.

Attributes that MUST place before

  1. Number
    2. isáng oras one hour
  2. As an Auxiliary Predicate Unit when there is an insert group* [pronoun and enclitics]
    3. Magalíng din siyáng coach He is a great coach as well.
    Notice here that the attribute connector attached itself after the insert group.
Attributes, Predicates, aren't they just adjective? Why complicate it?

Actually, adjectives are just the words specialised for describing things.

Attribute and predicate classification designates the description scope.

Also, it's not just adjectives that can be descriptions. A noun, a verb, a phrase, even a full sentence can be.

  • Haring Sejong King Sejong
  • boksingerong senador senator who is a boxer
  • tumatakbóng kabayo running horse
  • kabayong tumatakbo sa race track horse (that is) running on the race track
  • lalaking malakí ang ilong man whose nose is big
