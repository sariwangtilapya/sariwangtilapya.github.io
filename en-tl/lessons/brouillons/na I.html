Na

Not not the EpiCon « na » but SPAM.

What is to come is already come

It's 9 in the evening. You are with your friends and you declare "I'm hungry".
Well, it's 9 in the evening and you haven't eaten yet. Is one not supposed to get hungry with the passing of time?

What is bound to happen already happened.

Directly translating will result to "Gutom ako." but that would be completely wrong. In this situation, "na" should be used.

Gutóm na ako

You've been playing badminton with your friends for three hours. Tiring, yes. Thus you declare, "I'm tired."
Isn't it expected that one gets tired after a long period of play?

What is supposed to happen already happened.

Direct translation would give, "Pagod ako." but of course, "na" is to be used.

Pagód na ako

You're friend Johnny will be late to the party, he has things to do at work.
"Go ahead", he said, "I'll catch up."

You've started the party without Johnny. 45 minutes pass, enters the man. "I'm here", he declares.

Who is expected to come is already come.

Direct translation would give, "Nanditō 'ko." but like really? You're there? We've been expecting you.

Nandito na ako

A friend invited you for dinner. The dinner was delicious and very filling. Friend asked if you would like another serving.

"Thank you but I'm already full."

What is meant to happen has already happened.
