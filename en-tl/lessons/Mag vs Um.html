When is a verb "mag" verb or "um"

There are no solid rules but there are some rules solid enough.

This will not be a comprehensive list.

Mag if object-turned-verb

  • magbasketball
  • magtsinelas [wear] slippers
  • magtagalog [speak] Tagalog

Mag if borrowed straight from English

  • magretouch
  • magcompute
  • magbake
  • magdrive

Exceptions: gumraduate, gumo [traffic], cumonnect

Um if verb of displacement or state

  • pumuntá go to
  • lumabás go out
  • bumalik come back
  • tumayô stand up
  • umupô sit down

Mag if done to self, to own body parts or property

  • maghilamos wash face
  • maglinis clean/tidy up
  • maglutò cook
  • maghugas wash [body part, dishes, car]
  • magsaing cook rice

Um if natural phenomenon

  • umaraw [sun] shine
  • umulan rain
  • umbagyo storm
  • humangin [wind] blow

Um if adjective-turned-verb

  • gumanda beautiful/well
  • lumaki bigger
  • dumilím dark
