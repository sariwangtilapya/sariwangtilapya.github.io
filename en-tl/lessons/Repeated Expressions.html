*Repetition usually is limited to two syllables except when with epithet particle

Repeated personal pronouns

usually accompanied by enclitics e.g. lang, din

excludes other persons

  • kami-kami
  • sila-silá
  • kayu-kayó
  • tayu-tayo

Repeated question pronouns

indivualises/pluralises element

  • anu-anó which things
  • sinu-sinó which persons
  • saan-saán which places
  • alin-alín which things [from a selection]

Repeated period of time

indivualises element each

  • araw-araw every day
  • linggu-linggó every week
  • buwan-buwán every month
  • taun-taón every year

Repeated relative time

  • kani-kanina [lang] just earlier
  • maya-mayâ a bit later
  • bukas na bukas [din] tomorrow exactly

Repeated units

many a unit

  • tone-tonelada many a ton
  • kilo-kilo many a kilo

up to two syllables with hyphen

characterises having plenty of something

  • butas-butás having plenty of holes
  • tagpi-tagpî having plenty of patches
  • halu-halò having a lot of mixture components

After modal ang

  • any adjective in nude form

with epithet particle

when an object accentuates its own characteristic

  • karneng-karné so meaty
  • Triple H na Triple H having mannerisms very similar to that of Undertaker's
  • tatay na tatay having characteristics very associable to that of a father's

with epithet particle but

When one finds something⋅one accentuating that characteristic

  • bobong-bobó find someone really dumb
  • tangang-tangá find someone really dumb
  • sarap na sarap find something truly delicious

Uncontrollable urges

  • utút na utót find oneself in a situation in which one can no longer hold the urge to fart
  • ihing-ihî find oneself in a situation in which one can no longer hold the urge to pee
  • taeng-taé find oneself in a situation in which one can no longer hold the urge to poop
  • iyák na iyák find oneself in a situation in which one really needs to cry or can't hold tear

doesn't stop doing something

  • kain nang kain
  • iyak nang iyak
