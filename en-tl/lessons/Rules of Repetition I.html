First syllable repetition

In this repetition, only the first syllable is subject to repetition.

This repetition is mostly used for conjugation in the present and the future. Although it does have other uses.

*There are consonant blends that are not to be separated. Those that are pronounced as ch [ch, ts, ty, ti], sh [sh, sy, si], j [dy, di]

**«i» could be a part of a consonant blend. This is «i» is not the syllable's vowel

Hidden «i» before s-blend

Inherited from spanish but «i» instead of «e»

This «i» is not in the base form of the word but appears when repeated

The first syllable thus becomes, is
