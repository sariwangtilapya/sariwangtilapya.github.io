There is

There are two ways to express there is:

  1. Meron
  2. May

Then add the noun that there is after.
They mean the same thing. Although there two very important very little differences in construction.

For example, to say there is a cat

  1. Merong pusa¡
    There is a cat.
  2. May pusa¡
    There is a cat.

Notice that "meron" has been connected with the EpiCon

The distinguishing point of "may" is that it compounds with the noun. It is inseparable and doesn't attach to the EpiCon.
