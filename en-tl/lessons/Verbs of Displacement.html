These are verbs that indicate change of position from point A to point B.

  • puntá - go to
  • datíng - come / arrive
  • alís - go (out/away)
  • pasok - enter

  • labás - go out

Nominative pronouns

  • akó - first person (I)
  • siyá - third person (he/she)[Tagalog pronouns are NOT gendered]
  • ka - second person (you)

Motional Accusative case

The motional accusative case denotes directional location (point of origin, arrow of displacement, point of destination).
It is used only with verbs of displacement.
*Point of origin only applies to verbs that indicate leaving.

Examples in English:

  • leave work (point of origin)
  • climb the stairs (arrow of displacement)
  • cross the road
  • go home (point of destination)
  • enter the room
  • rotate 540°

The motional accusative case nouns must be preposited with « ng ».
For example:

  • Pumasok akó ng school - I went to school
  • Lumabás siyá ng CR - He/she came out the restroom
  • Lumayas siyá ng bahay - He/She left home (with intention of leaving the people in the house)
  • Umangát ng dalawáng level - It went up two levels
  • Sumakáy ka ng bus? - Did you ride a bus? (riding a bus to move not just being inside the vehicle)
