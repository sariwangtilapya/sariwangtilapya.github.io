Natural phenomena

  1. ulán - rain
  2. araw - sun
  3. hangin - wind
  4. bagyó - storm/typhoon
  5. bahá¡ - flood

The elements of the list above are all nouns.

In order to turn them into verbs, they must be conjugated. Once conjugated, they are already a complete sentence as natural phenomena don't take a subject.

We start with the past tense.

Insert « UM » after the first consonant. If it starts with a vowel, the nothingness before the vowel is the consonant.

  1. UMulán - It rained.
  2. UMaraw - It became sunny.
  3. hUMangin - It became windy.
  4. bUMagyó - It stormed.
  5. bUMahá¡ - It flooded.

Future tense

Take the first consonant + the first vowel, and then prefix it (add to start). If it starts with a vowel, the nothingness before the vowel is the consonant. If there is no vowel stress, then the vowel of the prefix must be.

´ on the final syllable indicates that there is no vowel stress. Unlike the unmarked default in which the penultimate syllable is stressed.

  1. Ūulán - It will rain.
  2. Aaraw - It will be sunny.
  3. HAhangin - It will be windy.
  4. BĀbagyó - It will be stormy.
  5. BĀbahá¡ - It will flood.

Present tense

Conjugate to future tense then conjugate to past

  1. UMŪulán - It's raining.
  2. UMAaraw - It's sunny.
  3. HUMAhangin - It's windy.
  4. BUMĀbagyó - It's stormy.
  5. BUMĀbahá¡ - It's flooding
