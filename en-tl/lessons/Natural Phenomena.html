Natural phenomena

  • ulán - rain
  • araw - sun
  • hangin - wind
  • bagyó - storm/typhoon
  • bahá¡ - flood
  • lindól - earthquake

The elements of the list above are all nouns.

In order to turn them into verbs, they must be conjugated. Once conjugated, they are already a complete sentence as natural phenomena are impersonal subjects. In English they are notated with an impersonal "it". We start with the

Past tense

Insert « UM » after the first consonant. If it starts with a vowel, the nothingness before the vowel is the consonant.

  • UMulán - It rained.
  • UMaraw - It became sunny.
  • hUMangin - It became windy.
  • bUMagyó - It stormed.
  • bUMahá¡ - It flooded.
  • lUMindól - There was an earthquake.

Future tense

The word must undergo "hydration", as in hydra, the mythological serpent which grows multiple heads.

Hydration is a very important step in many conjugations and declensions in Tagalog.
Here's how to hydrate:

  1. Take the first consonant.*
  2. Take the first vowel.**
  3. Prefix (add to start) steps 1 and 2.

*If it starts with a vowel, the nothingness before the vowel is the consonant.
**If the first syllable is not stressed, step 2 will be a long vowel.

  • Ūulán - It will rain.
  • Aaraw - It will be sunny.
  • HAhangin - It will be windy.
  • BĀbagyó - It will be stormy.
  • BĀbahá¡ - It will flood.
  • LĪlindól - There will be an earthquake.

Present tense

  1. Hydrate
  2. Infix « UM »
  • UMŪulán - It's raining.
  • UMAaraw - It's sunny.
  • HUMAhangin - It's windy.
  • BUMĀbagyó - It's stormy.
  • BUMĀbahá¡ - It's flooding
  • LUMĪlindól - There earth is shaking.

Hydration always takes priority. Drink water.

Additional vocabulary

  • mamaya¡ - later (within the day)
  • kanina - earlier (within the day)
  • bukas - tomorrow
  • kahapon - yesterday
  • next/last (week/month/year) - next/last (week/month/year)*

*Yes, this is not a mistake. Filipinos will say it as is in English.

You can place these relative time adverbs after the verb.
For example:

  • Lumindól last week. - There was an earthquake last week.
  • Ūulán mamaya¡. - It will rain later.

To make the sentence negative, use hindí¡.

  • Hindí¡ umaraw kanina. - The sun didn't shine earlier.
