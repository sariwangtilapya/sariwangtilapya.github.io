Adjectives

There are two basic types of adjectives:
  1. Simple
  2. MA

Simple adjectives are in the base form, while
MA adjectives are mostly nouns or sometimes verbs prefixed with MA to become an adjective.
Basically the equivalent of -ful in English

Most adjectives are MA adjectives

The Epithet Connector (EpiCon)

The EpiCon links the epithet to its described unit. The use of Epicon is obligatory.

What is an epithet?

The epithet is a qualitative word which has a local scope as opposed to a predicate.
This might not make sense to you so let's look these example sentences.

  1. The ball is red.
  2. the red ball

In both sentences, red describes the ball but only sentence 1 is a complete sentence. It is because "is red" is a predicate and predicates complete a sentence. A predical has sentence scope.
In sentence 2, red is just an epithet. An epithet has local scope.

Anyway, back to EpiCon

In Tagalog if a qualitative describes epithetically, the EpiCon must be placed between the desciptor and the described. Let's take the red ball example again

  • pulá - red
  • bola - ball

One can not just say 'pula bola'. That would be completely wrong.
There should be the EpiCon in between.

pulá (Epicon) bola

Epicon can take two forms

  1. na
  2. -ng

The default form is na BUT

  • if the word on the left ends in « n », remove it. Then prefix « ng »
  • if the word on the left ends in any vowel, prefix « ng »

pulá (Epicon) bola = puláng bola

Some other examples to analyse

  1. pantalong mahaba
    • pantalon - trousers / jeans
    • mahaba - long
  2. panís na kanin
    • panís - expired - spoilt
    • kanin - rice (cooked)
  3. green na t-shirt
  4. blue na libró
    • libró - book
  5. libróng blue

On the first example, if you noticed the noun came first. It's because it doesn't really matter that much anyway. The qualitative can come in either position and actually this is the more common structure. Except for numbers - they always come first, the common simple qualitatives have more chance that they come first, but everything else, they come after most of the time.

Try contructing the examples 1 and 2, the other way around.

Why did 3 and 4 not follow the rules?

Some words can just break them and take the default. Mostly because the word that preceeds the epicon is recognisably foreign and adding « ng » to its end can hurt the clarity of communication. Basically, don't be surprised when you hear or see a local break the rule.
