Types of nouns

There are two types of nouns :

  1. Proper and Personal (PP)
  2. Everything Else (EE)

The type of noun will determine with which particle must it accord.

The last lesson, we have learned that the nominative particle is « Si ».
But that is just for PP nouns.
For EE nouns, the nominative particle is either « Yung » or « Ang ».
In everyday speech, it's more common to use « Yung ».

The genitive case

Noun

The genitive case expresses possession.

PP Particle : « Ni »
TR Particle : « Ng » pronounced (nang)

Pronoun
Person Genitive
I Ko
II Mo
III N(i)yá
I pl ex Namin
I pl in Natin
II N(i)yó
III pl Nilá

Examples :

Doctor yung mother ni Mark
The mother of Mark is a doctor.
Businessman yung father ni Mary.
The father of Mary is a businessman.
Police yung husband ng sister ko.
The husband of my sister is a policeman.
Cousin ng dog ko yung dog mo.
Your dog is the cousin of my dog.
