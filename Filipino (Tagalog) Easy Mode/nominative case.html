The nominative case designates the subject of the sentence.

Nouns are always preceded by particles.
The nominative particle is « Si »
For two persons use pronoun + « ni ».
*Exception for 3rd persons use « Siná »

Pronouns (Nominative case)
Person Singular Plural
1st Akó
2nd Ka Kayó
3rd Siyá Silá
1st exclusive Kamí
1st inclusive Tayo
*3rd person is not gendered.

Examples:

There are no "be" verbs (am, is, are) in Filipino.
Predicate comes first, then the subject.

Nouns

Teacher si John.
John is a teacher.
Engineer kamí ni Samantha.
Samantha and I is are engineers.
Software developer sina Martin.
S/he and Martin are software developers.

Pronouns

Anong trabaho mo?
What's your job?
Bakit sa umaga siyá natutulog?
Why is it that s/he sleeps in the morning?
Veterinarian ka [diba]?
You're a veterinarian, [right]?
Student [pa lang] akó.
I'm [still] a student.
Security guard [kasi] siyá.
[It's because] s/he is a security guard.
