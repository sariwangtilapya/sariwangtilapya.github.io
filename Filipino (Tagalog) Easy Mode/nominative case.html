The nominative case designates the subject of the sentence.

Nouns are always preceded by particles.
The nominative particle is « Sì »
For two persons use pronoun + « nì ».
*Exception for 3rd persons use « Sinà »

Pronouns (Nominative case)
Person Singular Plural
1st Akó
2nd Ka Kayó
3rd Siyá Silá
1st exclusive Kamí
1st inclusive Tayo
*3rd person is not gendered.

Examples:

There are no "be" verbs (am, is, are) in Filipino.
Predicate comes first, then the subject.

Nouns

Teacher sì John.
John is a teacher.
Engineer kamí nì Samantha.
Samantha and I is are engineers.
Software developer sinà Martin.
S/he and Martin are software developers.

Pronouns

Anóng trabaho mo?
What's your job?
Bakit umaga siyá natutulog?
Why is it that he sleeps in the morning?
Veterinarian ka [dibá]?
You're a veterinarian, [right]?
Student [pà lang] akó.
I'm [still] a student.
Security guard [kasí] siyá.
[It's because] s/he is a security guard.
