Congratulations! You already have attained more than 50% progress in learning Tagalog just by knowing English as Filipino can be spoken with nouns, verbs and adjectives in all English – and some adverbs and disjuctions as well.

This course will teach Tagalog using English vocabulary in Tagalog grammar. Basically the bare minimum Filipino which could be still considered Filipino.
From here, one could start learning without having the lack of vocabulary as a stumbling block.

Although, please use the opportunity to learn vocabulary bit by bit while learning the grammar

Now onto the non-negotiables

  • Nominal Predicate + Nominative
  • Existence/Inexistence + Appartence/Non-appartenance — to have + Locative
  • Small Piece: You Have Not Spoken Filipino, You Speak No Filipino, Never Will You Speak Filipino
  • Verb: Indicative Mood + Nominative
  • Active Voice + Accusative
  • Passive Voice + Noun Definitivity
  • Dislocation
  • liste de quoi que ce soient
    • pa rín vs pa láng
    • na rín vs na láng
    • namán
    • tulóy
    • eh
