To express that there is something, use the structure
May + (something)

Example:

May rat.
There is a rat.
May tao.
There is someone.
lit. There is a person.
May kung anumán.
There is something.
lit. There is whatever-it-is.

To express that there is nothing of such, use the structure
Waláng + (something)

Example:

Waláng coffee.
There is no coffee.
Waláng tao.
There is no one.
lit. There are no people.

To express that there is nothing,

Walá¡.
There is nothing.

To express location, use the locative particle « Sa »
or locative pronouns

Locative pronouns

Near the 1st person Near the 2nd person Far from both
Dito D(i)yán Dún/Doón
*1st person takes priority if object is near both.
Here There Over there

It is very common to say the locative pronoun before the locative noun.

Example:

May cat dún sa roof.
There is a cat on the roof.
Waláng tao dito.
There is no one here.
There is no person here.
May yogurt dyán sa ref.
There's yoghurt on the fridge

To express appartenance/non-appartence, just add the possessor after the existence marker.

Example:

May ballpen ka?
Do you have a pen?
May pet dog si Joan.
Joanne has a pet dog.
May stocks ako sa Jollibee.
I have stocks on Jollibee.
