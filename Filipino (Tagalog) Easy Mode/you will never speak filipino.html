First of all, THERE ARE NO VERBS IN FILIPINO!!!

C'est-à-dire, there is not a set of words in the category of « verbs ».
Although, there are thousands of indefinite pronouns (someone, something, someplace, sometime, somehow…).

But how could it be possible that a language has no verbs?

There are verbs, of course. It's just that it's not a set of words; but a function.

Take for example « basketball ». What does one do with basketball?
Ride?
Listen?
Cook?
Of course not, you silly!
YOU PLAY BASKETBALL!

How about shoes?
Drink? Write? Sing?
NO! YOU WEAR IT!

In Filipino, one would say "Nagbabasketball ako" → "I'm basketballing", "Nagsapatos ako" → "I shoesed".

Now, the language, Filipino. I don't know — maybe it's edible or something.
Because in Filipino, it is not possible that "I speak Filipino" but possible that "I Filipino".

tldr; In Filipino, there is not word for speaking a language.

P.S. It is a common mistake to translate « speak » with « salita ». Salita means to talk or say something, not to speak a language.
